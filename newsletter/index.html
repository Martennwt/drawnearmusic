If God is good, why all the pain?

A few years ago I sat in a hospital corridor at two in the morning.

The vending machine hummed. Somewhere behind a door, a machine beeped for someone I love. And I remember thinking: if there is a God, this is the night I stop believing in him.

I had heard all the answers. Everything happens for a reason. It's all part of a plan. People mean well when they say these things. But down in that corridor, those sentences don't hold. They sound like a weather report read to someone whose house is on fire.

Here is what I only noticed much later.

When you're at the bottom of a pit, you don't actually want an explanation.
Nobody down there is asking for a theory.
You want someone to climb down.

That's the strange claim at the center of the old story I spent years avoiding. Not that God keeps a spreadsheet where all the pain adds up. But that he didn't stay outside of it. That he climbed down, all the way. Into the corridor. Into the beeping room. Into the grave. Whatever else you make of that story, it's the only version of God I've ever heard of who comes back with scars.

I still don't have an answer for the pain. I'm suspicious of anyone who says they do. I just stopped looking for an answer the night I realized what I was really looking for was company.

So here's my question, and I mean it honestly:

When your world last fell apart, what did you actually need: an explanation, or someone beside you?
